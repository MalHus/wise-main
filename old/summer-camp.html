WISE Summer Camp 2020

Overview

This summer, WISE UofT will be hosting a free online science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) summer camp for students aged 13-15! We’ll be hosting virtual interactive STEM activities over three days: August 15th, 16th, and 17th. Activities will include virtual biology labs, guided at home experiments, a fun coding workshop, a panel with amazing STEM leaders who are revolutionizing their field, and more! Whether you have had a passion for STEM your whole life or are just becoming curious, we encourage you to join us to get your STEM on, make new friends, and reflect on how a STEM career could be right for you!

Schedule

  • Day 1

    August 15th

    10:00 - 11:30am EST

    Workshop 1: STEM in the Summer - Panel Activity
    Have you ever wondered how the STEM field continuously advances? Who is behind this? How did they do it? One universal value of STEMist is their drive to make profound impacts on the world around us - from patient care to engineering a new technology. Come join us on August 15th from 10:00 AM to 11:30 AM EST for a chance to take part in an intimate conversation with a successful leaders in STEM, as they share the different paths they took and the obstacles they overcame in their respective fields! The workshop will give students the opportunity to ask questions and learn from panel members’ different experiences. And keep an eye out for our panelists - we’ll be introducing them over the next month!

    12:00 - 1:30pm EST

    Workshop 2: Have you heard about them… in STEM?
    You’ve heard of Elon Musk and Albert Einstein … but have you heard of Mae Jemison, Alice Ball, or Henrietta Lacks? Though these women brought about tremendous change in our society, their stories often go unheard due to the inherent prejudice in STEM-based fields. Drug discovery, car design, and AI algorithms shape our world, but the hidden biases deep within them directly impact our health, technology, and life. Come join us for an interactive workshop where we will learn about these great scientists, and explore implicit and explicit biases in STEM - from medicine to moonwalks!

  • Day 2

    August 16th

    10:00 - 11:30am EST

    Workshop 1: Kitchen Counter Genetics!
    Are you interested in DNA- the biological ‘string’ of letters that tells our bodies how to eat, sleep, and grow? The field of ‘genetics’, where DNA is scrutinized and studied, is crammed full of scientists who are revolutionizing healthcare, agriculture, environmental science and much more! But what does ‘genetics’ really mean, and how can you make it your career? In this workshop we will be exploring genetics at home by performing an experiment that you can do using household items! During your experiment you will be guided by a genetics expert through the process working with DNA from your own kitchen counter, while learning all about DNA, the field of genetics, and what genetics research is like in university and beyond!

    12:00 - 1:30pm EST

    Workshop 2: Virtual Laboratory Simulation
    Although you may imagine that a scientist typically wears a white coat and goggles, neither article of clothing is needed for this virtual lab! Using the virtual laboratory platform Labster, we will participate in a realistic simulated biological experiment. As a scientist, the skills you need to perform these experiments will be at the core of your career—whether you are researching which chemical compound forms the most efficient medicine or examining the mechanisms underlying a novel disease, you will surely encounter these lab techniques again!

  • Day 3

    August 17th

    10:00 - 11:30am EST

    Workshop 1: Math Empowerment Workshop: led by Vanessa Vakharia
    Despite the fact that girls and boys achieve the same level of success in math class, girls are disproportionately represented in post-secondary STEM programs and what’s crazier is that despite increasing test scores, young women still profess in large numbers to be BAD at math! This workshop explores the force exerting the most influence on teenage students: popular culture and mass media. Led by Toronto’s own Math Guru, Vanessa Vakharia, we will discuss how we might change math culture so that girls grow up with the understanding that math is just as cool as Kim K's butt, and DEFINITELY way more empowering!

    12:00 - 1:30pm EST

    Workshop 2: Coding Activity, Get With The Program(ming)
    Have you ever surfed through Google Earth and realized how cool it is to see the entire world from the comfort of your home? It is even more cool to discover that Google Earth was developed with the help of a young woman, Marissa Mayer! The development of not just Google Earth, but Google itself along with YouTube, Instagram, TikTok and all other websites are created using codes and programming. Through our workshop we will introduce you to coding and all the creativity that arises from it. You will be able to explore this creativity on your own through an interactive, animated coding session! There is nothing better than letting your imagination reach sky high while having fun at the same time! This workshop is run in partnership with Women in Computer Science UofT (WICS).
